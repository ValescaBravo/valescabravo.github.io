Customer Segmentation (RFM + PCA + KMeans)

I built actionable CRM segments by combining RFM signals with dimensionality reduction (PCA) and clustering (KMeans), producing activation-ready groups and clear recommendations for targeting and retention.

Problem

Business context

CRM teams need segments that are both statistically grounded and operationally actionable. The goal is to create customer groups that support prioritisation (who to focus on) and activation (what to say, when, and through which channel).

Data + method

How it was built

  • Features: customer-level RFM signals plus behavioural/channel indicators.
  • PCA: reduce dimensionality to stabilise clustering and improve interpretability.
  • KMeans: produce segments designed for CRM activation and prioritisation.
  • Profiling: compare segments by value, frequency, recency and engagement proxies.

Insights

3 key takeaways

  1. Clear value gradient: segments separate customers by value and engagement for prioritisation.
  2. Activation-ready rules: RFM-based characteristics translate into practical CRM targeting criteria.
  3. Channel signals matter: behaviour helps tailor offers and cadence (digital-first vs offline-led).

Recommendations

What I would do next

  • Design segment-specific CRM journeys (VIP retention, growth for mid-value, win-back for at-risk).
  • Refresh segments on a regular cadence (monthly/quarterly) and track migration across segments.
  • Use segment labels as features for response modelling and campaign optimisation.
